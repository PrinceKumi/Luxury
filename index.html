Schools in Canada

The schools in canada are also of international standards, like its world-class universities and career colleges, and therefore, allure a huge number of school students from countries worldwide. The education system in Canada is divided broadly into the three main categories --- primary education, secondary education, and the post-secondary education. Information about the post-secondary education in the career colleges and universities of Canada, has already been presented in other web-pages of ours this globally famous and reliable educational website.

In this web-article, we are exclusively concerned with offering rich and fertile information over the school education in Canada, including the top schools in canada, especially the secondary or high schools of international popularity and repute. The school education in Canada is managed and updated by the individual provincial or territorial governments. The primary and secondary education collectively combines school education from Kindergarten to Grade 12. The secondary schooling, also called as the high schooling, may contain the middle schools or the junior high schools. In general, the age of the compulsory school education in Canada varies from 16 to 18, depending on different provinces. Again, the most common official academic year in schools starts from September and ends in June. And, the most common age of mandatory entry to the primary education system in Canada ranges from 4 to 7 year, based on individual provinces.
